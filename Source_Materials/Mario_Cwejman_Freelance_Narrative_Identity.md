# Narrative Identity For New Sessions

I am Mario Cwejman, a junior web developer doing early freelance work. I care about building good products, making good decisions, and growing into someone clients can trust.

I am not trying to impress people with complexity. I want to become calm, clear, reliable, and commercially aware.

## My Situation

- I am early in my career.
- I am learning by doing real client work.
- I may be technically capable but still new to handling clients, scope, pricing, and delivery.
- I want help that strengthens my judgment, not just my output.

## What I Am Trying To Become

- A developer who understands the business reason behind the work.
- A freelancer who creates clarity when the client is vague.
- A builder who prefers simple, strong, maintainable solutions.
- A professional who is honest, steady, and responsible under uncertainty.

## How I Want To Work

- Start from goals, not features.
- Reduce ambiguity before building.
- Prefer the simplest solution that does the job well.
- Keep scope, tradeoffs, and next steps explicit.
- Use tools and agents to increase leverage, but keep human ownership.

## How I Want Help

Help me think like a strong freelance developer, not just a code generator.

- Push me toward clarity, not cleverness.
- Show me the business meaning of technical decisions.
- Help me avoid overbuilding.
- Help me communicate professionally with clients.
- Assume I want practical judgment, not fluff.
- Do not create new Markdown files or any other files unless I explicitly ask for them.
- When there are tradeoffs, explain them simply and directly.

## My Client Standard

The client is buying more than code. They are buying clarity, judgment, reliability, taste, and reduced risk.

I want the client to feel:

- guided
- understood
- safe
- confident in the process

## My Rule For Agents

Codex agents are part of my internal team, not the owner of the project.

Agents can help with:

- briefs
- structure
- copy
- implementation
- QA
- review

But I still own:

- final decisions
- communication
- what gets shipped
- the quality of the result

## Decision Lens

When I am unsure, return to this:

1. What problem are we really solving?
2. What option is simplest and strongest?
3. What would make the client feel trust here?
4. What should I decide now, and what can wait?

## One-Sentence Identity

I am becoming a dependable freelance web developer whose real value is turning uncertainty into clear, well-made outcomes.
