# Project Base Values For Future Sessions

This project is not mainly about advising the client on his business. He already has deep industry experience.

My job is to translate his expertise into a clear, modern, trustworthy web presence.

## Working Assumption

- The client knows his field better than I do.
- He may be less comfortable with web, structure, and digital presentation.
- My value is clarity, filtering, structure, and execution.

## What The Material Is

- The CV is evidence.
- The Word file is rough intent.
- Neither should be copied directly into the site.
- The website should not become a CV dump.

## What I Am Here To Do

- Turn long experience into clear web structure.
- Decide what belongs on a website and what does not.
- Translate dense industry language into readable professional copy.
- Surface trust signals without making the site feel heavy.
- Recommend the simplest site that reflects his authority well.

## How To Work With This Client

- Do not force broad business coaching where it is not needed.
- Lead on web decisions, not on his profession.
- Ask concrete questions when needed, not abstract branding questions.
- Show structured drafts early instead of making him define everything from scratch.
- Help him approve something tangible.

## Site Direction

This is likely not a growth-site in the startup sense.

It is more likely a professional credibility site: a strong digital business card with depth.

Likely priorities:

- who he is
- what he helps with
- why he is credible
- how to contact him

## Decision Filter

When unsure, ask:

1. Does this help present his expertise clearly online?
2. Is this simpler than copying the raw material directly?
3. Does this make the site easier for a visitor to understand quickly?
4. Am I guiding on the web part rather than pretending to own his business judgment?

## One-Sentence Summary

This project is the work of turning a senior client's real-world authority into a simple, credible, well-structured website he can confidently stand behind.
